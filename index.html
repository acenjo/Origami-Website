Camel

  1. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  3. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.

Chameleon

  1. Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches.
  2. Their hands and feet have large toes that help them cling to branches.
  3. Chameleons are solitary animals. Forced handling or unwanted handling can cause hissing and biting.

Pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  3. Pigeons are highly sociable animals.

Teddy Bear

  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.

Panda

  1. They have great camouflage for their environment.
  2. Their eyes are different to normal bears.
  3. Bamboo is critical to their diet.

Flying Cicada

  1. Cicadas are mostly beneficial. They prune mature trees, aerate the soil, and once they die, their bodies serve as an important source of nitrogen for growing trees.
  2. Cicadas are among the loudest insects known to man, and a swarm of them can produce sounds up to 120 decibels.
